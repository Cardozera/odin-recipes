Carbonara

Spaghetti Carbonara, a Classic Roman Delight

Spaghetti Carbonara is a timeless Roman pasta dish known for its simplicity and rich, creamy sauce. This Italian classic combines perfectly cooked spaghetti with a decadent mixture of eggs, cheese, pancetta, and black pepper, creating a dish that's both comforting and indulgent.

Ingredients

  • 400g (about 14 ounces) spaghetti
  • 150g (5 ounces) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup (100g) Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Steps

  1. Boil Spaghetti: Cook the spaghetti in a pot of salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve a cup of pasta cooking water before draining.
  2. Cook Pancetta: In a skillet, cook the diced pancetta (or guanciale) until it's crispy. Add minced garlic if desired.
  3. Prepare Cheese Mixture: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Pecorino Romano cheese, salt, and black pepper.
  4. Combine Ingredients: Toss the hot, drained spaghetti with the cooked pancetta. Remove the skillet from heat and quickly mix in the egg and cheese mixture. Add pasta cooking water gradually to create a creamy sauce.
  5. Serve: Garnish with chopped fresh parsley and additional cheese if desired. Serve immediately, savoring the creamy texture and bold flavors of this classic Roman pasta dish.
